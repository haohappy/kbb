Non-Statin Cholesterol-Lowering Drugs

1. PCSK9 Inhibitors
   - Evolocumab (Repatha), Alirocumab (Praluent)
   - Monoclonal antibodies that block PCSK9 protein
   - PCSK9 normally degrades LDL receptors on liver cells
   - By blocking PCSK9, more LDL receptors remain on the surface
   - Result: dramatic LDL reduction (50-70% additional when added to statins)
   - Expensive (~$5,000-14,000/year)
   - Given as subcutaneous injection every 2-4 weeks

2. Ezetimibe (Zetia)
   - Blocks NPC1L1 transporter in small intestine
   - Reduces cholesterol absorption from diet
   - 15-20% additional LDL reduction
   - Often combined with statins (Vytorin = simvastatin + ezetimibe)
   - IMPROVE-IT trial showed cardiovascular benefit

3. Bile Acid Sequestrants
   - Cholestyramine, Colestipol, Colesevelam
   - Bind bile acids in intestine, preventing reabsorption
   - Liver uses more cholesterol to make new bile acids
   - 15-30% LDL reduction
   - GI side effects common
   - Can raise triglycerides

4. Fibrates
   - Fenofibrate, Gemfibrozil
   - PPARα agonists
   - Primarily lower triglycerides (30-50%)
   - Modest HDL increase (5-15%)
   - Limited LDL effect
   - ACCORD trial: no CV benefit when added to statin for most patients

5. Niacin (Nicotinic Acid)
   - Inhibits hepatic DGAT2 enzyme
   - Raises HDL most effectively (15-35%)
   - Lowers LDL (5-25%) and triglycerides (20-50%)
   - Flushing is major side effect
   - AIM-HIGH and HPS2-THRIVE: no additional CV benefit over statin alone
   - Largely fallen out of favor

6. Bempedoic Acid (Nexletol)
   - ACL (ATP citrate lyase) inhibitor
   - Upstream of HMG-CoA reductase in cholesterol pathway
   - Prodrug activated only in liver (not in muscle → fewer muscle side effects)
   - 15-25% LDL reduction
   - CLEAR Outcomes trial (2023): CV benefit in statin-intolerant patients

7. Inclisiran (Leqvio)
   - siRNA targeting PCSK9 mRNA
   - Given twice yearly after initial doses
   - ~50% LDL reduction
   - Novel mechanism: RNA interference
