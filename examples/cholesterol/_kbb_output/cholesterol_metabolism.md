Cholesterol Metabolism: Why It Matters

Cholesterol is essential for:
- Cell membrane structure (fluidity regulation)
- Steroid hormone synthesis (cortisol, estrogen, testosterone)
- Bile acid production (fat digestion)
- Vitamin D synthesis
- Myelin sheath formation

Sources of Cholesterol:
- Endogenous synthesis: ~800mg/day (primarily liver)
- Dietary intake: ~300-500mg/day
- The body tightly regulates total cholesterol through feedback mechanisms

The Mevalonate Pathway:
Acetyl-CoA → HMG-CoA → (HMG-CoA reductase) → Mevalonate → ... → Cholesterol

This pathway also produces:
- CoQ10 (ubiquinone) - mitochondrial electron transport
- Dolichol - protein glycosylation
- Isoprenoids - cell signaling
- This is why statins can cause CoQ10 depletion

Lipoprotein Classes:
- Chylomicrons: transport dietary fat from gut
- VLDL: transport triglycerides from liver
- IDL: intermediate, between VLDL and LDL
- LDL: primary carrier of cholesterol to tissues ("bad cholesterol")
- HDL: reverse cholesterol transport to liver ("good cholesterol")
- Lp(a): genetically determined, independent CV risk factor

The Atherosclerosis Process:
1. LDL particles infiltrate arterial wall
2. LDL becomes oxidized (oxLDL)
3. Macrophages engulf oxLDL → foam cells
4. Foam cells accumulate → fatty streak
5. Smooth muscle cells migrate and proliferate
6. Fibrous cap forms over lipid core → atherosclerotic plaque
7. Plaque rupture → thrombosis → heart attack/stroke

Why Lowering LDL Works:
- Mendelian randomization studies show lifelong lower LDL = proportionally lower CV risk
- "Lower is better" - no lower threshold of benefit identified
- Each 1 mmol/L (39 mg/dL) reduction in LDL → ~22% reduction in major CV events
- This relationship is log-linear and consistent across populations
